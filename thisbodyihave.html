“This body layers and is layered, maps and is mapped, dreams and is dreamt. The story of what is underneath is not the truer version, or even the hidden version.” - Quinn Eades

‘This Body I Have’ is a surrealist investigation of my body, my relationship to it and my perception of it. Taking inspiration from the art movement of surrealism and its impact on fashion in the 20th century, combined with contemporary modes of surrealism and queer theory I attempted to create and represent the body I have and the body I wish I had. This exploration of gender dysphoria explained through art and literature is a theme within my work and acts as catharsis. Read more on my opinions on surrealism.

I acknowledge and pay my respects to the Woi wurrung and Boon wurrung language groups of the eastern Kulin Nations, the Traditional Custodians of the land on which I work. I also acknowledge that sovereignty was never ceded. I extend this acknowledgment and respect to all Aboriginal and Torres Straight Islander people of Australia and their Elders past, present and emerging.

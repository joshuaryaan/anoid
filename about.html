Anoid is a pseudonym for designer Connor Milton who is currently studying at RMIT in Naarm (Melbourne).

anoid
                       a.noid
                                               no ID
                                                                       paranoid
           android

The name anoid came from these words after a conversation with a friend about a personal identity crisis, a moment shared by many. This disjointed sense of self is explored further throughout works utilising the tangibility of garment design to link repetitively to the body. Specifically through the experience, function and visibility of the queer body within and outside of contemporary fashion discourse. Allowing the queer experience, queer community, art and literature inform design work makes for an encompassing approach to fashion practice with the intensions of exploration, service and homage to its inspirations and of its creator.

I acknowledge and pay my respects to the Woi wurrung and Boon wurrung language groups of the eastern Kulin Nations, the Traditional Custodians of the land on which I work. I also acknowledge that sovereignty was never ceded. I extend this acknowledgment and respect to all Aboriginal and Torres Straight Islander people of Australia and their Elders past, present and emerging.
